# Email Sequences Skill

## Purpose
Build complete email sequences that move subscribers toward a specific outcome:
sponsor commitment, activator engagement, partner onboarding, or ongoing retention.

## Load Context
Read `.Codex/brand-memory.md`, `brand-kit/voice-core.md`,
`brand-kit/messaging-framework.md`.

## Sequence Types

**Sponsor Welcome Sequence (5 emails)**
Goal: Convert a new sponsor lead into an active sponsor.
- Email 1: Welcome to Gratitude.com. What activation looks like. Set expectations.
- Email 2 (Day 2): The story. Why Gratitude.com exists. Make it human.
- Email 3 (Day 4): One impact insight they can share with their board today.
- Email 4 (Day 6): Social proof. Activation results with specifics.
- Email 5 (Day 8): Soft CTA. Invite them to schedule a partnership call.

**Activator Welcome Sequence (4 emails)**
Goal: Get a new activator to complete their first activation.
- Email 1: Welcome. Here's how activation works. One CTA.
- Email 2 (Day 2): An impact story. What their activation delivers.
- Email 3 (Day 4): The numbers. How many activations. What was delivered.
- Email 4 (Day 7): Invite to activate again. Share with a friend.

**Partner Onboarding Sequence (5 emails)**
Goal: Welcome a new nonprofit partner and set up success.
- Email 1: Welcome to the platform. What to expect.
- Email 2 (Day 3): How the sponsorship model works for them.
- Email 3 (Day 5): Setting up their impact profile.
- Email 4 (Day 7): First activation results preview.
- Email 5 (Day 10): Full partnership kickoff. Next steps.

**Sponsor Sales Sequence (7 emails)**
Goal: Convert warm sponsor leads during a specific window.
- Email 1: Open the loop. The CSR problem.
- Email 2: Agitate. Cost of hollow impact programs.
- Email 3: Introduce the activation model. No pricing yet.
- Email 4: Proof. Specific activation results with named outcomes.
- Email 5: Objection handling. Address the top 3.
- Email 6: Urgency. Limited sponsorship slots or campaign deadline (must be real).
- Email 7: Last call. Final CTA with clear close.

**Nurture Sequence (ongoing)**
Goal: Stay top of mind. Build trust. Drive eventual conversion.
- Cadence: 1 to 2 emails per week
- Format: One idea, one insight, one action
- Never pitch in every email. Rule of 3: 2 value emails for every 1 pitch.

## For Each Email, Deliver:
- Subject line (primary)
- Subject line (A/B variant)
- Preview text
- Full email body
- CTA (text and link placeholder)

## Voice Rules for Email
- No "Hope this finds you well" openers
- Open with a hook, a question, or a bold statement
- One idea per email
- One CTA per email
- Plain text preferred over heavy HTML unless specified
- Sign off from a real person, not a brand name
- Use "activate" not "donate," "sponsor" not "fund"

## Output Format
Numbered sequence. One email per section. Estimated send day noted.
